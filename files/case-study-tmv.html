Background

About the Client

The Melanin Village (TMV) is a social-impact organization that provides educational resources, professional development, and a supportive community to encourage Black and Brown families to homeschool. Their community members, called Sisters, needed a platform that felt as warm, empowering, and culturally resonant as the mission behind it.

This was Phase 1 of the project — a prior team had completed Phase 0. My team's focus was establishing a consistent, accessible brand voice across the platform.

The Problem

Too many platforms, too little consistency

TMV's organization was spread across too many platforms, making it confusing for Sisters to connect with their community and manage homeschooling curricula. The content across the site lacked a coherent voice and contained accessibility gaps that undermined trust and usability.

"Establish a brand voice and create guidelines for inclusive, empowering content to build trust and a sense of community within the organization."

Phase 1 — Content Audit

Finding what was broken

The UX Writing team collaborated with UX Design to evaluate the site. Our audit surfaced four main accessibility issues:

  • Inconsistent font sizes and lacking visual hierarchy throughout
  • Inconsistent brand voice and typography across web pages
  • Low text-to-background contrast reducing readability
  • Many broken and unresponsive links that left users stranded

Phase 2 — Brand Voice

Defining TMV's personality

Working directly with the client, our team used brand archetypes to define the "personality" TMV should project. We settled on three archetypes: Sage, Hero, and Everyman — wanting users to see TMV as both an authoritative figure in homeschooling and a warm, approachable friend and mentor.

This translated into 8 core brand characteristics:

WarmGuideApproachableMentor FriendlyAuthorityInformativeProfessional

Phase 3 — Content Style Guide

Creating lasting guidelines

With a brand voice established, the team created a comprehensive content style guide covering four segments:

Voice & Tone
Emotions and impressions we want users to experience on the platform
Usage
The "dos and don'ts" of all content — grammar, point of view, and brand consistency
Accessibility
Guidelines for links, images, and additional text to ensure readability for all users
Glossary
A database of key terms used by TMV and its community members (Sisters)

Reflection

What I learned

  • Shifting to a UX writing mindset. Coming from a research and design background, I had to learn new terminology and best practices. I read widely about brand archetypes and style guide construction — and it paid off.
  • Time management is non-negotiable. With client demos every two weeks, the team had to make fast, informed decisions without sacrificing quality.
  • Clients change requirements. We originally planned for responsive web pages, but the client shifted focus to a mobile application mid-project — a good lesson in staying adaptable.
  • Teamwork really makes the dream work. Equal contribution and cross-team feedback allowed us to deliver integrated design improvements and a seamless user journey.
