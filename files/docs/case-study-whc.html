Background

About the Client

We Help and Care (WHC) is a nonprofit charity supporting humanitarian and development assistance in Southeast Asia — particularly in Myanmar, where communities face urgent and ongoing crises. This project was Phase 0, meaning the goal was to lay a strong research foundation that future teams could build upon, not to ship a final product.

"This is only Phase 0 of the project. We will be setting up a research foundation for future phases."

The Problem

Low engagement, low trust

WHC was struggling to attract user engagement and membership. The website had numerous inconsistencies in content display and outdated material, leaving users with negative impressions and confusion about the organization's legitimacy and purpose.

Goal 1
Validate the problems being solved and identify the highest-priority opportunities
Goal 2
Define a foundational vision, product strategy, and target audience
Impact
Increase membership, secure stable donations, and raise awareness of Myanmar's humanitarian crisis

Phase 1 — Site Audit

Identifying areas of improvement

The UX Research team conducted a thorough audit of WHC's main web pages. Our findings clustered into four problematic domains:

  • Information: Lack of updated content and unclear expectations for membership responsibilities.
  • Technical: Unfinished code visible to users; multiple languages displayed throughout the site.
  • Design: Buttons and links appeared disabled; photos and text were used inconsistently across pages.
  • Copywriting: Numerous grammatical errors with an odd tone not tailored to the audience or to the people WHC serves.

Phase 2 — User Testing

Empathizing with potential donors

We conducted user tests with participants who had a history of making donations. Our research questions focused on:

  • What helps people decide whether to get involved in charities?
  • How do users interact with WHC's current site?
  • What is the site doing well, and what is it missing?

User testing revealed behavioral and emotional patterns we couldn't have predicted from the audit alone:

"I'm not sure if this organization is still active because the most recent project was beginning in 2022."

"Who are they helping? What families? Is this around the world? I still don't know."

"[These are] well-chosen pictures. They summarize it all very well!"

Research Findings

6 key insights, 6 recommendations

01
Achievement metrics leave a strong, positive impression — add them strategically.
02
Photos are a powerful engagement tool — add more, curated intentionally.
03
The scope of WHC's mission is unclear — make who they help unmistakably clear.
04
Outdated content signals untrustworthiness — separate current from past projects.
05
Multi-language display creates confusion — pick one language based on user preference.
06
Social media buttons were ignored — make them visible and explain their purpose.

Next Steps

Handing off to Phase 1

The team prepared a full documentation package to enable the Phase 1 team to hit the ground running, including a final site audit report, the Phase 0 research plan, and a user testing final report. Phase 1 will expand the team to include UX Design, UX Writing, Product Strategy, and Web Development, with goals of conducting additional usability tests, building low-fidelity wireframes, and creating design and content style guides.

Reflection

What I learned

  • Recruit the right participants. We chose people experienced in donating and using technology — so when they struggled with the site, we knew it was a real usability issue.
  • Photos carry enormous weight. They gave the site a human touch and conveyed emotions that regular text simply couldn't.
  • Watch faces, not just actions. Where users' eyes wandered during testing — staring while squinting — revealed confusion that verbal feedback never would have surfaced.
